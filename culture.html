Traditions

The Philippines is a culturally diverse place and has many different traditions, 4 of which being:

  • Bayanihan
  • Fiestas
  • Four-month Christmas

In the Philippines, the Bayanihan spirit is one of communal unity, being selfless towards others, to achieve a certain goal. Bayanihan happens when Filipinos unite to do heroic acts for individuals and communities in need without expecting anything in return. Giving aid to anyone, including strangers, is Filipino culture. In times of need, when everyone is a victim, Filipinos pitch in and give whatever they can. Magbayanihan tayo is a Filipino phrase used when someone invites other to become heroes for the community. In this case, a hero is someone who loves his people.

The Philippines having being colonised by Spain for more than 300 years, a large number of Filipino citizens are Catholic/Christians. Most provinces have a patron saint and celebrate fiestas or festivals in their honour. The biggest and most elaborate festival of all is Christmas. The fiesta is a lot more than it seems, it can bond Filinpinos from a region or an area together and a time to reunite with you extended family.
Some of the more popular Philippine Fiestas: Ati-Atihan, Pasungay, Sinulog.

Christmas is a special tradition in the Philippines. In the Philippines, Christmas lasts multiple months, lasting the duration of the months from September to January. These are called the "Ber" months. Families typically gather together to celebrate on December 25, but the holiday season starts earlier with lighting up neighborhoods and malls around November. Christmas Carols are played in shopping centers and people buy new clothes and shoes to wear in church or parties. "Noche Buena", or Christams Eve, is when most families eat their biggest meal of the year. After dinner they open presents. Some Filipino families also observe Simbang Gabi, a series of nine dawn masses on the days leading up to Christmas - beginning on December 16 and ends at midnight on December 24, when a midnight mass is held.

Music & Dance

The Philippines is a country with many cultural influences, including Spanish and American music and dance. One popular form of Filipino music is the kundiman, which is often sung in Tagalog. The kundiman has a slow melody that reflects the sadness of love lost or unrequited. Another type if Filipino music is the sinatraa, which features fast-paced rhythms and lively melodies.
One popular Filipino dance style is the tinkling. The dancers imitate the movements of two bamboo sticks clacking together as they hop between them. Other popular dances include the zapateado (a tap dance), balintawak (a folk dance from Luzon), and harana(a courtship dance).
Karaoke is also a very significant part of the Philippine culture. When growing up in the Philippines, you are brought up surrounded by singing. When looking to pass time, karaoke is a popular activity of everyday life. If visitors come to the house, they will all sing together, or if it's a speical occasion they will enjoy singing as part of the celebrations.

Sports

Filipino sports are a big part of the culture in the Philippines. Basketball is the most popular sport, followed by boxing and football. The Philippines has produced some world-class athletes, like Manny Pacquiao.
Basketball is considered a major sport in the Philippines. There are many professional basketball leagues in the country, including two that play in international competitions: The Philippine Basketball Association (PBA) and Maharlika Pilipinas Basketball League (MPBL). The PBA was founded in 1975 and is currently composed of 10 teams. It is one of Asia's oldest professional leagues. The MPBL was founded in 2018 and has 24 teams competing nationwide.
Boxing is also a very popular sport in the Philippines, with many world champions coming from there, like Donnie Nietes. Football is another popular sport; both American football and association football (soccer). The Philippine Azkals are the men's national soccer team who have qualified for multiple AFC Asian Cup tournaments.
